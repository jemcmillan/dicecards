Refine and Focus

Goal

The goal during this part of the DEFINE stage is to refine and focus the research and insights you've collected and use that information to write a problem statement or set of problem statments.

Creating clear, concise, specific, actionable, and human-focused problem statements is the most important part of the DICE Model. Without clear and consise problem statments, the ideation phase may not produce real solution options, the creation of content and experiences will be unfocused and may not be purposeful, and evaluations tend to be focused on outputs, not outcomes.

Writing a good problem statement

A good problem statement should:

  1. Be phrased as a question. Problem statement that start with "How might we..." or "What can do to..." help teams come up with more creative solution ideas in the Ideation phase of the DICE Model.
  2. Not impose limitations. Even though there may be many obvious limitations, from time available to available tech or money, building in constraints now could impede good ideas in the Ideation phase.
  3. Be specific. Identify one issue or problem only. Be specific about the people the problem affects.
  4. Avoid naming specific solutions. Avoid naming specific solutions. Be award of hidden soltions like "Problem: Increase cyber training in order to reduce successful phishing attacks." Even though that problem doesn't name a specific solution to "increase training" it assumes "increasing training" is the solution for decreasing phishing attacks.
  5. Be human-focused, not organization-focused. Frame the issue as a human problem, not an organization issue. Instead of: "How can the ship lower the number of Sailors clicking dangerous web links?" Instead, focuson human behavior: "How can we help Sailors better recognize dangerous web links?"
