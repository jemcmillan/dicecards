Content Layering

What is it?

Content layering is the practice of “layering” relevant and complementary topics and linking them within your content.

Why use it?

Using content layering as part of your content strategy will help increase the authority of the content, increase brand awareness and help increase audience size.

  • Shared authority. The authority, accuracy and reliability of one piece of content will be shared by the next. For example, a subject matter expert used in a story will add to the credibility of content linked to it.
  • Increased brand recognition. With well-planned and well-executed branding, the same, easy-to-recognize characteristics will help in associating content among multiple information products. Layering content that use the same branding indicators will help users recognize the organization or campaign brand.
  • Increased audience size. If one type of content is proving successful and has gained a large audience, promoting another product within the already successful content will lead to more viewers for the other product. Additionally, by layering content - and ensuring each layer is search engine optimized - you create multiple entry points to your content.
