Develop the Plan

Communication plans can be simple, complex or anywhere in between. No matter what level of complexity they take, all communication plans should contain the following:

Problem statement. Include the problem statement or set of problem statements.

Objective. Write SMART objectives.

  • Specific. What is to be done? "How will you know it is done?" and describes the end product of the work to be done. The description is written in such a way that anyone reading the objective will most likely interpret it the same way.
  • Measurable. How will you know it meets expectations? Enand defines the objective using assessable terms like quantity, quality, frequency, costs, deadlines, etc. It refers to the extent to which something can be evaluated against some standard.
  • Achievable. Can the objective be accomplished? Does the team have the experience, knowledge or capability to fulfill the expectation? Can it be done giving the time frame, opportunity and resources?
  • Relevant. To ensure the objective is relevant ask: 1) Should it be done? 2) Why should it be done? 3) What will be the impact? and 4) Is the objective aligned with the organization's strategic plan?
  • Time-oriented. When does the objective need to be done? An objective needs to have end points and check points built into it. Sometimes a task may only have an end point or due date.

Sub-objectives. Sub-objectives are sets of SMART objectives that support a main objective.

Define audiences. Specifically define your target audiences. Who's support is needed to achieve the objective? Who are you trying to inform, influence or motivate?

Identify tools. What tools will you use to achieve the objective? What content will need to be created? What experiences need to be designed?

Establish a timetable. Once all these elements have been considered, and preferably documented, develop an overall timeline that can be followed.

Evaluation plan. The best communication plans also include an evalution plan. Take reviewing the Evaluate section of this site to determine what evalutions best fit the specific communication plan you're designing.
