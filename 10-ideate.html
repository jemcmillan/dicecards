Mindset: Convergent Thinking

The convergent-thinking stage is where ideas are evaluated, compared, ranked, clustered or ditched in an attempt to pull together a few great ideas to act on. The aim is spotting the best ideas, or combinations of idea attributes, from a number of ideas.

Before selecting the best proposed solutions, you should take time to identify and list likely constraints. Up to this point in the IDEATION phase, you've been working in a world where there were no constraints. Now is the time to consider those constraints. Are there limits to:

  • Time
  • Money
  • People
  • Technology
  • Support

Idea Selection Criteria

  • Does it fit with people's needs? Is there demand?
  • Does it really meet the goals set initially?
  • Does it meet the requirements in our problem statement?
  • Does it answer our “How Might We” questions in a satisfying way?
  • Is it different enough from what exists to add additional value?
  • Do we have access to the budget—enough to implement even partially?
  • Can we get approval from decision makers?
  • Can this idea become a habit or a trend?
  • Does the idea fulfill a practical need?
  • Will we be able to roll it out with the available resources?
  • Is the technology available?
