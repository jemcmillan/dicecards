Feedback Forms

Feedback forms are a type of survey. Feedback forms can be printed forms customers/users complete in person or can be digital forms online or on a stand-alone kiosk.

Feedback forms can incorporate written-response questions and/or ratings-based questions such as a Likert scale or a max differential scale.

The best customer feedback methods meet three criteria:

  • Are convenient for customers to use.
  • Help customers feel comfortable about providing feedback.
  • Focus on the attributes that are most important to the customer.

Three Important Questions

  1. “How likely is it that you would recommend our product/service to a friend or colleague?”

    This question is called the Net Promoter Score (NPS) question. It gives you deep insight into what your customers think about your brand.

  2. "How do you rate your last experience with us?”

    HelpScout.net writes that news of bad customer service reach more than twice as many ears as praise for a good service experience and that for every customer who bothers enough to complain, 26 other customers remain silent.

    Asking this question will allow you to learn your customer thinks about your service, gives you an opportunity to offer a solution to a problem, and lets your customers tell you about their feelings.

  3. “If you could change just one thing about our product/service, what would it be?”

    This question helps you determine your customers’ pain points. It also shows your customers that you care about their opinion and their voice is important to you.

    4. Likert Scales

    Likert scales are survey questions that offer a range of answer options — from one extreme attitude to another, like “extremely likely” to “not at all likely.” Typically, they include a moderate or neutral midpoint.

    Example:

    "Overall, how satisfied or dissatisfied are you with our company?"

    • Very satisfied
    • Somewhat satisfied
    • Neither satisfied nor dissatisfied
    • Somewhat dissatisfied
    • Very dissatisfied

    Likert scales (named after their creator, American social scientist Rensis Likert) are quite popular because they are one of the most reliable ways to measure opinions, perceptions, and behaviors.

    When to use a Likert Scale?

    Likert scales are great for digging down deep into one specific topic to find out (in greater detail) what people think about it. Use a Likert scale any time you need to find out more about:

    • How people react to your content
    • How people felt after attending an event
    • What people think about (insert topic)
    • Any question where you need to measure sentiment about something specific and you want a deeper level of detail in your responses.

    Max Differential Scales

    MaxDiff is an approach for obtaining preference/importance scores for multiple items (brand preferences, brand images, product features, event preferences, engagement preferences, etc.).

    Research has shown that MaxDiff scores demonstrate greater discrimination among items and between respondents on the items. MaxDiff questions are written so they are simple to understand, so respondents from a variety of educational and cultural backgrounds can provide reliable data. Since respondents make choices rather than expressing strength of preference using some numeric scale, there is no opportunity for scale use bias. This is an extremely valuable property for cross-cultural research studies.

    Example:

    Which of the following displays/booths would most most likely make you choose to attend an airshow?

    Most Important Least Important
    Blue Angels performance
    WWII Aircraft aerial performance
    Aircraft cockpit simulator
    Real Aircraft cockpit photobooth
    Parachute team demonstration

    MaxDiff Benefits

    • Strong discrimination power
    • It is a simple task for the respondent
    • Allows to test a larger number of items
    • Eliminates scaling bias
    • Allows for diversity, which is necessary in international studies
    • Provides ratio data and a measure of magnitude
