5E Model

A smart guide to help design better experiences is Andy Sontag’s 5E Experience Design Model (5E). This tool can help you align a team, access collective creativity, enable collaboration and design in features to increase coherence, elegance and excitement within an experience.

Starting with a high concept idea, you can use the 5E Design Experience Model to start building the experience by designing all the touchpoints where participants will interact with the experience.

  1. Excitement. The way we attract participants to our experience.
  2. Entry. Entering the experience, crossing from one context into the designed context.
  3. Engagement. The activities that immerse the participant(s) in the experience.
  4. Exit. The clear end of the designed experience.
  5. Extension. A physical or digital object that the participant can take with them to “extend” the experience.
