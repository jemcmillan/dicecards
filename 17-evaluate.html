Collect Measurements

Performance measurements are used to assess an activity’s performance against its objectives and to identify which elements of the activity were most and least successful.

There are five types of performance measurements:

  1. Inputs. The actual activity carried out.
  2. Outputs. The results of the inputs.
  3. Out-Takes. The immediate effect on the target audience.
  4. Intermediate outcomes. What the target audience did as a result of your activity.
  5. Outcomes. Whether or not the objective was achieved.

    6. Source: Government Communication Service of the UK

Inputs

Inputs include details of the actual activity that has been undertaken.

Examples include:

  • Number of direct engagements
  • Number of stories released (by type)
  • Number of press releases distributed
  • Number of experiences/engagements conducted
  • Number of tours conducted (by type)

Outputs

Output metrics measure the number of people who had the opportunity to see or hear your activity, regardless of whether they recall or recognize it.

Try to include:

  • Reach – the total number of people or organizations in your target audience who were exposed to your activity.
  • Frequency – the number of times they saw or heard the activity.

Examples include:

  • Number of people reached by each engagement
  • Publication circulation
  • Story impressions
  • Number of inquiries from the news media due to press release
  • Number of people reached by experiences/engagements (attendance)

Out-Takes

Out-take metrics look at the impact the activity had on your target audience’s awareness, understanding and attitude. Think about what you wanted people to recall, think or feel about your activity and include performance metrics that allow you to measure this.

Examples include:

  • Recall: How many people can recall a specific message?
  • Recall: How accurately can someone recall a specific message?
  • Think: How many people understood the key message(s)?
  • Think: What does the target audience not understand consistently?
  • Feel: What was the content source’s (news organization) coverage towards the issue before they were exposed to the communication activity?
  • Feel: Does the target audience feel they will now behave differently after being exposed to the communication activity?

Intermediate Outcomes

Intermediate outcome metrics capture actions taken by the target audience as a result of your activity. These actions may lead to the eventual desired end behavior.

Examples include:

  • Talk Are more people talking about the activity/message?
  • Direct Response How many people engaged leadership or SMEs in direct face-to-face conversations about the activity/message?
  • Indirect Response How many people are interacting with associated third parties? This could be people seeking information or having discussions with stakeholders, partners, or other local or national services.
  • Other Actions How many people took, or claim to have taken, action on the issue/message?

Outcomes

Outcome metrics measure whether or not the Objective and Sub-Objectives were met.

If we use the examples from the Ideation phase, we have a main objective:

  • Decrease the number of DUIs from our organization by 50% over the next six months.

And we have three sub-objectives:

  • Sub-Objective 1: Increase the number of team members who know how to use the organization's “Safe Ride” program to 75% over the next six months.
  • Sub-Objective 2: Decrease team members’ reported reluctance to use “Safe Ride” program, taxi cabs or ride-share programs to 25% over the next six months.
  • Sub-Objective 3: Increase willingness of peers to intervene to stop team members (known and unknown) from driving while under the influence of alcohol to 90%.
