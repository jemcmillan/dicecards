Communication Box Model

Goals

To communicate holistically to the target audience by helping planners consider the four approaches in the box model:

  1. Direct communications
  2. Influencer communications
  3. Passive communications
  4. Expereinces and Engagements

How to Use

Direct communication

This is direct contact with the audience. It affords the opportunity for two-way communication. It allows the communicator to get verbal and non-verbal feedback. It includes:

  • Face-to-face conversations
  • Meetings
  • Training sessions
  • Speeches and talks
  • Question and Answer sessions
  • Social media conversations

Passive communication

This approach involves indirect contact with the intended audience. This approach is considered passive because even though communication products have been delivered to the audience, the audience chooses to receive it and the communicator isn't sure the audience understands it. This approach includes:

  • Posters
  • Television spots and stories
  • Radio spots and stories
  • Social media posts with no back-and-forth engagement
  • Email and snail mail marketing

Influencer communication

This approach involves targeting people with influence on the intended audience's actions or opinions. Use the other three communication approaches to engage influencers effectively.

Experiences

This approach involves engaging the audience in an activity that provides them a perspective on the end goal. This approach includes:

  • Hands-on demonstrations
  • Interactive displays
  • Tours and visits
  • Games
