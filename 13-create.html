Mindset: Convergent Thinking

Create Content

Dictionary.com defines content as "something that is to be expressed through some medium, as speech, writing, or any of various arts." Graphic arts, photographs, videos, written stories and multimedia pieces are all content. Online posts, to include those on blogs, micro-blogs, and other social media, are also content. Content also includes printed materials, messages disseminated via digital boards or other means, and any other digital or physical form of communication.

Four Reasons for Content

There are four major reasons to create content:

  1. To inform
  2. To persuade
  3. To motivate
  4. To build greater understanding

Content Value

Content is valuable when it reaches the intended audience in the most appropriate form and achieves a communication goal. Content is most valuable when it is delivered to the audience in the manner they prefer. People typically want their news and information in one of two ways:

  1. As fast and as easy-to-digest as possible
  2. As in-depth and as easy-to-understand as possible

Fast and Easy-To-Digest Content Fast and easy-to-digest content is typically mobile, social, visual and shareable. It can also be analog. Printed posters, simple infographics, and other graphic forms can communicate fast and be easy-to-digest. Animations, live content, photos and news stories and videos can also deliver fast and easy-to-digest information.

In-Depth and Easy-To-Understand Content In-depth and easy-to-understand content is typically delivered through written, audio, video or multimedia features. It can also be delivered through detailed and data-driven infographics, live content and audio or video documentaries or feature series.

Content Principles

Start with User Needs Write in a way that suits the situation. Ask yourself: Who is going to read, watch or listen to this? What do they need to know? How might they be feeling? Help people find the information they need quickly and easily? Guide them through the process.

Make it simple. Use plain language and simple sentences. Choose clarity over cleverness.

Write for everyone. Respect the complexity of our users’ experiences. Be willing to be surprised about who’s reading your work.

Build trust. Talk like a person. Tell the truth. Use positive language and concrete examples.

Start small and iterate. Make sure your content works for users. Don’t be afraid to scrap what’s there and start over. Write a draft, test it out, gather feedback, and keep refining.
