Creating the High Concept

What is it?

The "high concept" idea comes from the entertainment industry where a succinctly stated, easily understood premise for a creative work is needed to pitch to producers and publishers.

A high concept is an intriguing idea that can be stated in a few words and is easily understood by all.

High Concept Examples

  • Toy Story. What if toys could think for themselves?
  • Inception. Your mind is the scene of the crime.
  • The Hunger Games. Fight to the death on national television.
  • Independence Day. What if aliens invaded Earth?
  • Groundhogâ€™s Day. Stuck living the same day over and over again.

Create a High Concept Idea

Ask "What if? sk "What if?" questions that put your target audience as the protagonist of a story or event.

  • What if... people could experience how dangerous texting and driving is?
  • What if... people could feel it feels to fight a fire?
  • What if... people could play with compound interest and see how it works over time?

Each of those "what if" statements could be worked into a high-concept:

  • A car simulator that tests your reaction time to hazards.
  • Tours into a controlled fire fighter trainer.
  • An app that simulates your savings account over time.

High Concept's are:

  • Universal. It must resonate with everyone.
  • Unique. It must be unique or has a fresh twist on a familiar idea.
  • Short. It should be summed up in a few words.
  • Has a great title.
