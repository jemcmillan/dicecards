Effective Coordination

Coordination

Coordination employs two important concepts: order and equality. Coordination can be defined as: Causing parts to function together and Working together efficiently.

Coordination is:

  • Sharing information
  • Discussing issues and possible courses for action
  • Achieving consensus on objectives and overall strategy
  • Adopting specific responsibilities and tasks in the context of the agreed objectives and strategy

Coordination has the following features:

  1. Group effort: Coordination integrates the efforts of individuals and organizations to make them work as a group.
  2. Unity of action: Coordination ensures unity of action between individual and organizational activities. It ensures that activities of each individual and group are headed towards the common goal.
  3. Common goal: Each individual and group strives to maximize its goal. Coordination maintains balance between individual and group goals.

Coordination Activities

Sharing baseline information on:

  • Roles and responsibilities
  • Resources and capabilities
  • Types and quantity of capabilities
  • Type and quantity of assistance
  • Areas of operations
  • Priority needs
  • Projects and gaps
  • Local context

Working together at:

  • Assessing needs
  • Setting standards for assistance
  • Mobilizing resources
  • Ensuring access
  • Building capacities
  • Training together

Steps to Effective Coordination

  • Central office in charge
  • Mutually agreed reasonable expectations
  • Mutually agreed roles and responsibilities
  • Exchange of liaison officers
  • Inter-agency or inter-departmental, inter-group meetings

To Coordinate with Partners

  • Explain what you can do and want to achieve
  • Listen to what they can do and want to achieve
  • Decide together on activities and responsibilities
