Mindset: Convergent Thinking

Analyze and Report

After the activity is completed, you will need to measure how effectively it met its objectives.

Begin by gathering data and evidence from all your sources and bringing it together in a centralized database, spreadsheet or folder.

Check to see whether it looks correct before beginning the analysis. Also, check that the activity ran as planned and whether anything unexpected is likely to have affected its performance.

How to Approach Analysis

Did your activity work as you expected?

Your evaluation plan mapped out how you expected your activity to work. Use this as the basis for your analysis.

Create some key hypotheses — results or outcomes you might expect to see based on your objectives, activity map and past performance.

Check performance against your sub-objectives. Have the sub-objectives been met? If so, what is driving success? If not, consider whether your targets were realistic and whether your sub-objectives are accurate measures of success.

Objectivity

Analyze the full range of potential outcomes and results for your activity. Do not ignore results or trends that don’t fit with your hypotheses or with the general patterns in the data. Instead, look for the reasons behind these. If something didn’t work in the way that you expected or failed to meet its objectives, look at the reasons why. This will help you to amend future activity and so improve future performance.

Isolate issues

Did any operational issues or external issues affect your activity’s performance? For example:

  • Did the operational work schedule affect any portion of the data collection?
  • Did any of your distribution methods experience any issues? (E.g., internet outages, email issues, printer problems.)
  • Did external factors like the weather or other news have an impact on your activity’s success? (E.g., snow days limited number of people at work, a major news/event took attention away from your campaign.)

Draft Report

Whatever format your report is expected to be in, try to follow these principles:

  • Be objective and include all results, positive and negative.
  • Always provide clear conclusions and recommendations — what should be done as a result of this report and by whom?
  • Separate fact from opinion and recommendations so that others can review the evidence on which you have based your conclusions.
  • Acknowledge gaps in the data — and their implications.
  • State any assumptions that you have made, and what you based them on.
  • Include references for all data sources used in your report.
  • Include appropriate graphs and images to aid interpretation.
